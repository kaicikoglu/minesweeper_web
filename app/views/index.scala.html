Heritage of Minesweeper

Minesweeper is a classic single-player puzzle game that originated in the 1960s. It gained widespread popularity after being bundled with Microsoft Windows operating systems starting from Windows 3.1. The game's simplicity and addictive nature have made it a timeless classic enjoyed by millions around the world.

The objective of Minesweeper is to clear a rectangular board containing hidden mines without detonating any of them. The game requires logical thinking and strategic decision-making to reveal safe squares and avoid the mines.

Rules of Minesweeper

The rules of Minesweeper are straightforward:

  • The game is played on a grid of squares, some of which contain hidden mines.
  • Players click on squares to reveal what is underneath. If a mine is revealed, the game ends.
  • Numbers on revealed squares indicate how many mines are adjacent to that square.
  • Using the numbers as clues, players must deduce the locations of mines and flag them to avoid detonation.
  • The game is won when all non-mine squares are revealed.

How to Play Minesweeper

Playing Minesweeper is easy! Here's a simple guide to get you started:

  1. Left-click on a square to reveal what's underneath.
  2. Use the numbers to deduce the location of mines.
  3. Right-click on a square to flag it as a potential mine.
  4. Avoid clicking on mines to keep the game going.
  5. Win the game by revealing all safe squares!
